Section 1: Email Drafting

Copilot helps you draft emails faster and with the right tone.

Accessing Copilot in Outlook

New Email:

  1. Click "New Email"
  2. Click the Copilot icon in the toolbar
  3. Or press Alt + I to open Copilot

Replying:

  1. Open an email
  2. Click Reply
  3. Click Copilot icon → "Draft with Copilot"

Where to find Copilot in Outlook:

  • New email: Copilot icon appears in the message toolbar (typically a purple sparkle icon)
  • Replying: After clicking Reply, look for Copilot icon in the compose window toolbar
  • Reading pane: Copilot icon may appear at the top of the email or in the toolbar

Drafting a New Email

Basic prompt:

Draft an email to the engineering team about the upcoming 
onboarding wizard launch. Include the launch date (Feb 15),
key features, and ask them to review the final checklist.

With specific tone:

Draft a professional email to the VP of Sales updating them 
on Q1 product roadmap progress. Be concise and focus on 
business impact. 3 paragraphs max.

Exercise 1: Draft a Stakeholder Email

Do this now:

  1. Open Outlook and start a new email
  2. Click the Copilot icon
  3. Enter this prompt:
Draft an email to my manager with a weekly product update.

Include:
- Onboarding wizard is 80% complete, on track for Feb 15
- Blocked on API integration, need engineering support
- User research completed, 3 key insights to share
- Ask for 15 minutes to discuss roadmap priorities

Tone: Professional but conversational
Length: 4-5 sentences
  1. Review the draft
  2. Try adjusting it with follow-up prompts

Adjusting Drafts

After Copilot generates a draft, you can refine it:

Change tone:

Make this more formal

Make this friendlier and more casual

Change length:

Make this shorter - 3 sentences max

Expand this with more detail

Add elements:

Add a bullet list of the action items

Add a clear call-to-action at the end

If Copilot generates more paragraphs than requested, follow up with: "Shorten this to exactly 3 paragraphs — combine the middle sections."

You may see playful options like "Make it a poem" or "Make it funnier" in the Copilot suggestions panel. These are standard Copilot options available to all users — simply ignore them and use the tone/length adjustments that fit your enterprise communication needs.

Exercise 2: Tone Adjustment

Do this now:

  1. Use the email draft from Exercise 1
  2. Try these adjustment prompts:
Make this more concise - half the length

Add a clear call-to-action asking for a meeting

Make the tone more urgent

Section 2: Email Summarization

Long email threads are a PM reality. Copilot summarizes them instantly.

Summarizing Threads

  1. Open a long email thread
  2. Click the Copilot icon at the top of the email
  3. Or look for "Summary by Copilot" automatically displayed

Summarization Prompts

Basic summary:

Summarize this email thread

Focused summary:

Summarize this thread focusing on:
1. What decisions were made
2. What's still open
3. What action items exist

Find specific info:

What did Sarah say about the timeline?

Action-focused:

What action items are mentioned in this thread?
Who is responsible for each?

Copilot may not capture every action item in very long threads (100+ messages). For complete accuracy, ask Copilot to process sections: "Summarize only the last 10 messages first."

Exercise 3: Thread Summarization

Do this now:

  1. Find a long email thread (5+ messages)
  2. Open it and click Copilot
  3. Try:
Summarize this thread in 3 bullet points

What decisions have been made in this thread?

Are there any open questions that need answers?

What action items are mentioned and who owns them?

Catching Up on Inbox

When you're behind on email, Copilot helps you triage:

Inbox Catch-Up Prompts

In a specific thread:

I haven't read the last 5 messages. What do I need to know?

Finding urgent items:

Is there anything in this thread that requires immediate action from me?

Preparation:

What context do I need to reply to this effectively?

Section 3: Calendar & Meeting Prep

Copilot helps you prepare for meetings from Outlook.

Meeting Prep

Before a meeting, Copilot can help you prepare:

  1. Open the calendar event
  2. Click Copilot
  3. Ask for preparation help

Meeting Prep Prompts

General prep:

Help me prepare for this meeting. What should I know?

Based on emails:

Summarize recent emails related to this meeting topic

Agenda creation:

Based on the meeting invite, suggest an agenda with 
time allocations for a 30-minute meeting

Questions to ask:

What questions should I prepare for this meeting?

Exercise 4: Meeting Preparation

Do this now:

  1. Open an upcoming meeting in your calendar
  2. Click Copilot
  3. Try:
Help me prepare for this meeting:
1. What's the context based on recent communications?
2. What should I bring or prepare?
3. Suggest 3 questions I should ask

Email Templates for PMs

Save these for quick use:

Stakeholder Update

Draft an email to [stakeholder] with a project update:
- Project: [name]
- Status: [on track/at risk/blocked]
- Key updates: [2-3 points]
- Next steps: [1-2 items]
- Ask: [what you need from them]

Tone: Professional, concise

Meeting Request

Draft an email requesting a meeting:
- Purpose: [topic]
- Duration: [time needed]
- Urgency: [timeline]
- Proposed times: [suggest flexibility]
- Context: [brief background]

Feedback Request

Draft an email asking for feedback on [document/feature]:
- What it is: [brief description]
- What feedback you need: [specific asks]
- Deadline: [when you need it by]
- How to provide feedback: [instructions]

Status Report

Draft a weekly status report email:
- Accomplishments: [this week's wins]
- In progress: [current work]
- Blockers: [issues needing help]
- Next week: [upcoming priorities]
- Asks: [what you need]

Outlook + Teams Together

The real power comes from combining Outlook and Teams Copilot:

After a meeting:

  1. Get meeting summary from Teams Copilot
  2. Draft follow-up email in Outlook Copilot
  3. Reference meeting decisions and action items

Before a meeting:

  1. Summarize related email threads in Outlook
  2. Check Teams chat for recent context
  3. Prepare with full picture

Best Practices

Do:

  • ✅ Always review drafts before sending
  • ✅ Adjust tone for your audience
  • ✅ Use summarization for long threads
  • ✅ Prep for meetings using Copilot
  • ✅ Save effective prompts for reuse

Don't:

  • ❌ Send Copilot drafts without review
  • ❌ Trust summaries for critical decisions without checking
  • ❌ Over-rely on Copilot for sensitive communications
  • ❌ Forget to personalize automated drafts
  • ❌ Skip context when asking for drafts
