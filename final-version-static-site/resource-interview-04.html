Stakeholder Interview #4: New Hire (First-Year Analyst)

Participant: Aisha Thompson Role: Analyst, Strategy & Consulting Location: Boston, MA Tenure: 8 months at Accenture Current Project: Healthcare payer transformation Products Used: AKX (trying to use), ADH (timesheets only) Date: February 10, 2025 Interviewer: Senior Product Owner Duration: 30 minutes

Background

Aisha is a first-year analyst who joined after completing her undergraduate degree. She's eager to learn and perform well but has struggled with the learning curve around internal tools. This interview provides perspective on the onboarding experience and challenges facing new hires.

Interview

Q: Tell me about your first experience with AKX.

Aisha: "Honestly? Overwhelming. During onboarding they showed us this tool, said 'all our knowledge is here,' and then moved on. My first week on a project, my manager asked me to find a healthcare market sizing framework. I went to AKX, typed it in, got hundreds of results. I had no idea which one to use. Are they all good? Are some outdated? I spent two hours and eventually just asked a colleague who emailed me their favorite template."

Q: How do you use AKX now, 8 months in?

Aisha: "I mostly don't. I've learned it's faster to ask someone than to search. My team has a shared folder with 'the good stuff' - templates, frameworks, examples they've collected over time. That's my go-to. AKX is sort of a last resort when no one knows what I need."

Q: What would help you use AKX more effectively?

Aisha: "Curation. Tell me what's good. When I search for something, show me 'Top 5 recommended' or 'Most used this year.' Have someone - maybe the practice - tag content as 'approved' or 'gold standard.' Right now everything looks the same. A random meeting note and a polished client deliverable both show up as search results. I can't tell the difference."

Q: How about finding experts or mentors?

Aisha: "I tried once. I wanted to find someone in healthcare AI because I'm interested in that area. I searched in AKX, found some names, but I had no idea who was approachable or who might be willing to mentor a junior person. I was too intimidated to cold-email a Managing Director. If there was something like 'open to mentoring' or 'available for coffee chats,' that would help."

Q: What's your experience with ADH?

Aisha: "I use it for timesheets. That's literally it. I enter my hours, submit my expenses. The interface is not intuitive - took me weeks to figure out where things were. I've heard ADH has information about projects and staffing but I've never explored it. No one showed me how."

Q: How was the overall onboarding experience with these tools?

Aisha: "There was a lot thrown at us during onboarding - too much to absorb. For the tools, it was basically 'here's what it is, here's how to log in.' No hands-on practice, no real use cases. By the time I actually needed to use AKX for a project, I'd forgotten everything from onboarding. Trial and error is how I've learned."

Q: What do you think would help new hires?

Aisha: "Real examples. Show me: 'You're an analyst, here are the 5 things you'll search for most often, here's how to find them.' Make it role-specific. And have some kind of 'starter kit' - pre-bookmarked content for new analysts, recommended templates, key documents. Don't throw me into a system with millions of documents and expect me to figure it out."

Q: Anything else you'd like us to know?

Aisha: "My peers and I talk about this. We all have the same experience - AKX is overwhelming, ADH is confusing. We've all developed workarounds. Maybe that's normal? But it feels like we're working around tools instead of with them. And when I compare to friends at other consulting firms, they seem to have an easier time with their internal systems."

Key Insights

  • Onboarding introduces tools but doesn't teach practical usage
  • First search experience was overwhelming - hundreds of results, no quality signals
  • Developed workaround: team shared folders instead of AKX
  • Needs curation: "Top recommended," "Most used," "Gold standard" indicators
  • Expert discovery intimidating for juniors - no signals about approachability or mentorship availability
  • ADH used only for timesheets - other features unknown
  • Wants role-specific guidance: "You're an analyst, here's what you need"
  • Suggests "starter kit" concept for new hires
  • Peers share same frustrations - universal new hire experience
  • Compares to friends at other firms who "seem to have an easier time"
